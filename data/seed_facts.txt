1. COMPANY INTERVIEW ROUND STRUCTURES
------------------------------------------------------------------------

1.1 Amazon SDE-1 (India campus) [VARIES — round count fluctuates 3-5]
    - Typical loop: 1 Online Assessment (2 DSA problems + work-style/
      behavioral simulation, ~70-90 min) → 1-2 technical coding rounds
      → 1 Bar Raiser round.
    - Bar Raiser: conducted by a senior interviewer from a DIFFERENT
      team, with veto power over the hire. Evaluates the candidate
      against Amazon's 16 Leadership Principles (e.g., Customer
      Obsession, Ownership, Dive Deep, Earn Trust) using STAR-format
      behavioral questions, sometimes mixed with an additional coding
      question. Round length: 45-60 minutes.
    - Full process (application to offer) can take anywhere from 3
      weeks to several months depending on role and panel availability.

1.2 Google SWE University Grad / L3 (India campus) [STABLE structure]
    - Loop: 1 online coding assessment → 2 DSA/coding interviews →
      1 "Googleyness & Leadership" behavioral round → (system design
      generally NOT part of the L3 new-grad loop; it starts at L4).
    - Googleyness round: 45 minutes, STAR-format, evaluates comfort
      with ambiguity, collaboration, and learning mindset — Google's
      answer to "culture fit," deliberately less rigid than Amazon's
      Leadership Principles.
    - After the loop, a Hiring Committee (not the interviewers)
      reviews the full packet and scores across 4 dimensions: General
      Cognitive Ability, Role-Related Knowledge, Leadership, and
      Googleyness. This review typically takes 1-2+ weeks.
    - Team matching now happens before the Hiring Committee review.

1.3 Microsoft SDE-1 (India campus) [VARIES]
    - Typical loop: Online coding round → 2 technical interviews
      (DSA + CS fundamentals) → 1 "As Appropriate" (AA) round with a
      senior engineer, which can include behavioral + technical depth
      and sometimes has informal veto-like weight similar to a
      Bar Raiser.

1.4 Flipkart SDE-1 [VARIES]
    - Typical loop: Online assessment → 2 DSA-focused technical
      rounds → 1 Hiring Manager (HM) round covering project depth and
      behavioral fit.

1.5 Infosys (InfyTQ / off-campus) [STABLE]
    - Path: InfyTQ certification test (Python or Java) → Technical
      interview → HR interview.
    - Infosys HR rounds are known to filter out otherwise technically
      qualified candidates on communication clarity — treat it as a
      real evaluation, not a formality.

2. ELIGIBILITY / CGPA CUTOFFS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
[VARIES — service companies publish official cutoffs; product
companies mostly do NOT. Numbers below are the commonly reported
range, not official policy, except where marked OFFICIAL.]

2.1 TCS — OFFICIAL, verified against TCS's published eligibility
    doc: minimum 60% aggregate (≈6.0 CGPA on a ×10 scale) across 10th,
    12th, and graduation, with a maximum of 1 active backlog at the
    time of the selection process (must be zero at joining).

2.2 Infosys, Wipro, Cognizant, Capgemini, HCL, LTIMindtree: commonly
    require the same 60% / 6.0 CGPA threshold across 10th/12th/
    graduation as TCS.

2.3 Amazon, Microsoft: do not publish an official CGPA cutoff for
    India campus hiring. Commonly cited informal floors are around
    6.0-6.5 CGPA, but average SELECTED candidates tend to sit in the
    7.5-8.5 range. Product companies weight DSA/coding performance
    far more heavily than CGPA.

2.4 Google: has stated it has no hard CGPA cutoff globally. In
    practice, campus resume shortlists at top-tier colleges skew
    toward 7.5+, but strong DSA candidates below that have gotten
    through — CGPA is a soft signal, not a gate, at Google.

2.5 Flipkart and similar product/unicorn companies: informally cited
    around 6.5 CGPA where a threshold exists at all.

2.6 Important nuance for the assistant to convey: CGPA×10 formula
    differs by university (e.g., Anna University/VIT/SRM/current-
    scheme VTU use CGPA×10 directly; AKTU/SPPU/older-scheme VTU use
    (CGPA−0.75)×10). A student should convert using their own
    university's formula before assuming eligibility — don't state a
    single CGPA number as universally equivalent to a percentage.

3. APTITUDE / ONLINE TEST PATTERNS
------------------------------------------------------------------------

3.1 TCS NQT (National Qualifier Test) — 2026 integrated format
    [VARIES year to year, verify each season]
    - Total: ~190 minutes, two mandatory parts, ~82-92 questions
      depending on source.
    - Foundation section (~75 min, ~65 Qs): Numerical Ability, Verbal
      Ability, Reasoning Ability — determines Ninja-band eligibility.
    - Advanced section (~115 min): Advanced Aptitude (~15 Qs, 25 min)
      + Advanced Coding (2 problems, 90 min) — determines Digital/
      Prime-band eligibility.
    - NO negative marking anywhere in the test (confirmed for 2026).
    - Question locking: once you move to the next question you cannot
      go back; switching browser tabs ends the exam immediately.
    - Highest eligible band (Ninja / Digital / Prime) is offered
      automatically based on percentile performance — you don't apply
      to a specific band separately.

3.2 Infosys InfyTQ: certification test in Python or Java → clearing it
    is the gateway to the technical interview, then HR.

3.3 Wipro NLTH (National Level Talent Hunt): Aptitude + English +
    Online Technical Test + Coding round, broadly similar structure to
    TCS NQT but company-specific weighting.

4. DSA PRIORITY LIST (for product-based company interviews)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
[STABLE — reflects consistent patterns across recent interview
reports]

Must-know (near-certain to appear): Arrays, Strings, Linked Lists,
Binary Search, Recursion.

High-priority: Dynamic Programming (1D/2D, knapsack variants), Trees
(BST operations, LCA, traversals), Graphs (BFS/DFS, shortest paths),
Sliding Window, Two Pointers.

System design basics needed even at SDE-1/L3 level (more likely to
surface as a discussion within a coding round than as a dedicated
round for true freshers): Load Balancers, CDN, Caching (Redis), SQL
vs NoSQL, Horizontal vs Vertical scaling. Note: for Google L3
specifically, a DEDICATED system design round is generally NOT part
of the new-grad loop — don't over-index prep time here for Google
freshers specifically; it matters more for Amazon/Microsoft/Flipkart
SDE-1 and starts becoming a real Google round at L4.

5. RESUME RULES (for freshers)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
[STABLE — general industry consensus]

- 1 page maximum, reverse-chronological order.
- Action verb + metric format: not "worked on API performance" but
  "Reduced API response time by 35% by implementing Redis caching."
- Section order: Education → Technical Skills → Projects →
  Internships → Achievements.
- Avoid generic self-descriptors like "hard-working" or "team player"
  as skills — list actual technologies, tools, and frameworks instead.
- Triple-check dates and project timelines; interviewers do catch and
  probe inconsistencies.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
6. PLACEMENT PROCESS CONCEPTS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
[STABLE]

- PPO (Pre-Placement Offer): a full-time offer extended at the end of
  an internship, before the main campus season. Counts as a confirmed
  placement — the student generally skips the regular process for
  that company. Strong, measurable internship outcomes plus visible
  manager feedback are the main levers for earning one.
- Campus recruitment season: roughly August-December for Tier-1
  companies, November-March for Tier-2/3 and mass recruiters — exact
  windows shift year to year and by college, so treat this as a
  rough seasonal pattern, not a fixed calendar.
- Mock interview slots are commonly opened by placement cells in the
  1-2 weeks before a company's scheduled campus visit.
