The campus recruitment season in Indian engineering colleges follows a distinct timeline divided by company profiles. Dedicated placement cells coordinate the entire cycle, which usually begins with Tier-1 companies like Amazon, Google, and Microsoft arriving to recruit roughly between August and December. Following this initial phase, Tier-2 and Tier-3 corporations, alongside mass recruiters such as TCS, Infosys, Wipro, and Cognizant, typically conduct their placement drives from November to March. Throughout these months, final-year students participate in company pre-placement talks, clear rigorous aptitude tests, and advance through multiple technical and HR interview rounds to secure their engineering roles.

Amazon's SDE-1 interview process is structured to assess a candidate's technical skills, problem-solving abilities, and alignment with the company's values. It begins with an online assessment consisting of two data structures and algorithms (DSA) problems and a work-style simulation. Successful candidates then move on to one or two technical rounds where deeper technical knowledge is evaluated. The final stage is the Bar-Raiser round, a unique aspect of Amazon's hiring process. In this round, an interviewer from a different team evaluates candidates against Amazon's 16 Leadership Principles using the STAR format. The Bar Raiser has veto power and ensures candidates meet Amazon's high standards.

Google's L3 Software Engineer University Graduate interview process is designed to gauge both technical prowess and cultural fit. It starts with an online coding assessment, followed by two interviews focused on data structures and algorithms. These technical rounds are intended to assess a candidate's problem-solving skills. The process culminates in a "Googleyness & Leadership" behavioral round, lasting 45 minutes. In this round, interviewers use the STAR format to evaluate a candidate's fit with Google's culture and leadership potential. Unlike higher-level interviews, a dedicated system design round is not part of the L3 new-grad loop. Finally, a Hiring Committee reviews the candidate's full interview packet across four dimensions: General Cognitive Ability, Role-Related Knowledge, Leadership, and Googleyness.
